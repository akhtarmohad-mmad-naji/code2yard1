This Privacy Policy was last updated on September 5, 2021.

1. What Data We Get

At Codeyard, we take your privacy seriously. Whether you’re visiting our website, signing up for our services, or engaging with us in any way, we want to ensure that your personal information is protected and that you’re fully informed about how we handle your data. Our Privacy Policy is here to explain how we collect, use, and safeguard your information. Let’s break it down so you know exactly what to expect when you interact with us.

1. What Information Do We Collect?

When you visit our website or use our services, we may collect two types of information:

  • Personal Information: This includes any data you willingly provide to us, such as your name, email address, phone number, and payment information. For example, when you contact us or sign up for our newsletter.

  • Non-Personal Information: This includes data that’s automatically collected when you interact with our website, such as your IP address, browser type, and website usage patterns. This helps us improve your experience and tailor our services to better meet your needs.

2. How Do We Use Your Information?

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Providing Services: We use your personal information to offer, manage, and improve the services you request from us, such as website design, development, and maintenance.

  • Communication: We may use your email address or phone number to respond to your inquiries, send service-related updates, or notify you about promotions and offers (if you’ve opted into them).

  • Improvement and Analytics: Non-personal information helps us enhance our website’s functionality and content, ensuring we provide you with the best possible user experience.

  • Security: We use your data to protect our website and services from unauthorized access, fraud, and abuse.

3. Do We Share Your Information?

We value your privacy and do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. However, there are certain situations where sharing data is necessary:

  • Third-Party Service Providers: We may share your information with trusted partners who help us deliver services (such as payment processors or hosting providers). These partners are contractually obligated to protect your data and use it only for the services we provide.

  • Legal Obligations: If required by law, we may disclose your information to comply with legal processes, protect our rights, or investigate fraud.

  • Business Transfers: In the unlikely event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred, but we’ll notify you and ensure your data is still protected.

4. How Do We Protect Your Information?

We implement a variety of security measures to protect your personal information. This includes:

  • Encryption: Sensitive data, such as payment information, is encrypted using secure protocols.

  • Access Control: We restrict access to your data to authorized personnel only, and we take steps to prevent unauthorized access or disclosure.

  • Secure Hosting: Our website is hosted on secure servers, and we continuously monitor for potential vulnerabilities.

While we make every effort to keep your information secure, it’s important to remember that no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely 100% secure.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

To improve your experience on our website, we use cookies and similar technologies. Cookies are small files stored on your device that help us remember your preferences, track site usage, and improve the content and functionality we offer. Here’s what we use cookies for:

  • Website Functionality: Ensuring the website works smoothly and remembers your settings.

  • Analytics Tracking how visitors interact with our website so we can improve our content and services.

You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences, but please note that disabling cookies may affect your experience on our site.

6. Your Rights Regarding Your Data

As the owner of your personal data, you have the right to:

  • Access: You can request to see the personal data we have on file for you.

  • Update: You can correct or update any inaccurate or incomplete personal information.

  • Delete: You can request that we delete your personal information, subject to certain legal obligations or business needs.

  • Opt-Out: You can unsubscribe from our promotional emails at any time by clicking the “unsubscribe” link in our emails.

If you have any questions or wish to exercise your rights, please contact us at the details provided below.

7. Children’s Privacy

Our services are not intended for individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors. If we learn that we have collected data from a child under 18, we will promptly delete it.

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Any updates will be posted on this page with the revised date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed.

At Codeyard, we are committed to protecting your privacy and providing a safe, secure online experience. We believe in transparency, and we want you to feel confident when you interact with us. If you have any questions or concerns about how your information is handled, don’t hesitate to contact us. Your trust is important to us, and we’re here to ensure your data is kept safe and secure.
